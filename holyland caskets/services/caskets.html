Types of Caskets:

  • Metal Caskets
    • Of the many metals used to make caskets, those in bronze or copper are usually the most expensive and prestigious. They are rust proof, considered to be of the highest possible quality and usually priced by weight per foot. However, because metal caskets cannot be used for cremation, they are not as commonly found in Singapore.
  • Wooden Caskets
    • These are more commonly seen in Singapore funerals and can be made of various types of hardwood, from Mahogany to Oak or even Veneer. Of these, the Mahogany, Cherry and Walnut ones are usually the most prestigious and expensive while those made with Pine, Poplar or Veneer are normally the cheapest.
  • Green Caskets
    • In line with the Green movement worldwide, Green Caskets, made of eco friendly materials such as bamboo, hemp or organic wood are now available in western countries. We are currently studying the feasibility of bringing these caskets into Singapore.
  • Gasketed vs Non-Gasketed Caskets
    • There is a misconception that gasketed (or sealed) caskets can preserve the body better. In reality, all they do is keep out water, dust and debris. Gas will still freely enter and escape and hence, decomposition will still take place.
  • Interior Lining of Caskets
    • The interior is often lined with either velvet, satin or cotton crepe. There is also a variety of colours to choose from.
  • Burial or Cremation Caskets
    • If the Casket is being used for cremation, metal caskets as well as caskets with metal parts are automatically out.
