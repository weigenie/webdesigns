Buddhist Funeral Services Singapore


In accordance to Buddhist beliefs, a funeral is meant to be a simple, dignified and solemn expression of bereavement. To prepare the body for the funeral, it must first be bathed and clothed. To a Buddhist, death therefore marks the beginning of a new round of rebirth, determined by the both the quality and quantity of karma accumulated during a deceased lifetime. For the family, other than providing the deceased loved one with a meaningful and dignified sendoff, the funeral serves to help him move on smoothly to his next life. The funeral will also serve as a good reminder for all present of the transient nature of life.
